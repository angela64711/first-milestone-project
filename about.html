Who I am

Doula portrait

I’m Evita — a multilingual, internationally experienced doula.
With a background in Biology and Neuroscience and years of research experience across Greece, Switzerland, and California, I bring evidence-based knowledge alongside deep compassion to my work.

My story

The birth of my daughter, Zoé, in 2019 marked a profound turning point in my life.
It deepened my understanding of birth as a transformative rite of passage and led me to follow my true calling: supporting families with presence, compassion, and informed care.
As a doula, I support people in advocating for their needs, navigating complex systems and experiencing pregnancy and birth with confidence and autonomy.
My role is not to replace medical care, but to complement it—offering continuous emotional presence, practical support, and space for informed decision-making throughout pregnancy, birth, and the postpartum period.
Before becoming a doula, I worked as a researcher in Biology and Neuroscience. This background shapes my evidence-based approach and my respect for both the science and the lived experience of pregnancy and birth.
Having personally navigated PCOS, infertility, pregnancy loss, and amenorrhea, I bring both professional knowledge and lived understanding to my work. I hold space for these experiences with empathy, respect and understanding.
I offer doula support in person in Berlin and online.
For parts of the year, I am also available in Zurich and Athens.
